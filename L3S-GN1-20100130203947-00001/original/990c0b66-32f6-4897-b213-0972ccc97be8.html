2008 will get off to cold start

Tuesday, January 01, 2008
By KIM LANIER
Staff Reporter

Some of the coldest air of the season is expected to blast the region with arctic force, bringing a hard freeze all the way to the coast by Wednesday night.

The surge of cold air is expected to drive temperatures in Mobile down into the middle 20s tonight into Wednesday morning, according to forecasters.

Although readings throughout most of southwest Alabama should bottom out in the low to mid-20s tonight into Wednesday morning, winds should remain strong enough to keep the region just above hard-freeze warning criteria, with less than five hours below 26 degrees, forecasters at the National Weather Service in Mobile said Monday.

Those blustery winds are also expected to produce wind chills in the single digits to the middle teens for several areas tonight into Wednesday morning, weather officials said.

The coldest conditions by far should arrive Wednesday night into Thursday morning, when lighter winds and clear skies should allow for a widespread hard freeze. Weather officials issued a hard-freeze watch for Wednesday night and Thursday morning across southwest Alabama.

Lows are expected to dip to near 20 degrees at Mobile Regional Airport, according to Kirk Caceres, a meteorologist with the National Weather Service in Mobile.

Temperatures in Daphne could drop to near 21 degrees Wednesday night into Thursday morning, while a low near 29 is expected at Dauphin Island. Meanwhile, inland areas such as Evergreen and Brewton could plummet into the teens, Caceres said.

For Thursday night into Friday morning, warmer conditions should make their way back into the region, with lows in the 30s expected near the coast, Caceres said. Forecasts call for readings in the mid- and upper 20s in inland areas.

As of Monday afternoon, several area hardware stores reported no increase in customers seeking space heaters, pipe insulation or protective coverings for plants. However, an employee at one store expected a slight increase Monday just before closing time, once residents got off work and saw the latest forecasts.

Gail Thomas, a manager at Lowe's home improvement store on the East Interstate 65 Service Road, noted that firewood was still in good supply.

With the store closing early and residents still in holiday mode, folks likely hadn't really thought much about coming into the store to prepare for the cold, Thomas said.

"There'll probably be a lot of people" today, she said.

The Waterfront Rescue Mission on State Street expects a rush of people seeking shelter from the cold tonight and Wednesday night.

When temperatures are predicted to drop below 40 degrees for three or more hours, the shelter relaxes its rules to take people in, even if they have exceeded their limit for overnight stays during the month, according to Rick Powell, an evening chaplain with the rescue mission.
