Erekat: Exceptions to Israeli settlement freeze not to be accepted
www.chinaview.cn 2007-12-29 19:34:15   Print

    Special report: Palestine-Israel Relations

    RAMALLAH, Dec. 29 (Xinhua) -- Palestinian negotiator Saeb Erekat said on Saturday that the Palestinian National Authority (PNA) would not accept any exceptions to Israeli settlement freeze in the West Bank.

    "The obligation before Israel is that the settlement activities, including the natural growth, come to a halt. So, there must be settlement or no settlement... there is no room for exceptions," Erekat told local radio Voice of Palestine.

    The Palestinians were angered by Israeli tenders to expand Har Homa settlement in the disputed east Jerusalem area. The tenders came after Annapolis peace conference, which set to restart Israeli-Palestinian talks on final status.

    Press reports say Israel was going to make concessions on settlement activities in the West Bank ahead of U.S. President George Bush's visit to the region in January. But Har Homa expanding tenders seem to be excluded from the concessions." Excluding Abu Ghneim Mountain (Har Homa) from the freeze can't be accepted," Erekat reiterated.

    He called on the U.S. administration, which hosted Annapolis peace forum in November, to take the initiative to make sure that all Israeli settlement expansion would be frozen. Meanwhile, Erekat revealed that Israeli and Palestinian negotiating teams will meet again in the coming couple of days to begin to tackle the final status issues.

    The two sides relaunched their peace talks on Dec. 12 as a result of Annapolis but have failed to yield any tangible progress.
