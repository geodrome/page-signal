Canoeist's wife in police custody

Press Association
Sunday December 9, 2007 8:48 PM

The wife of back-from-the-dead canoeist John Darwin is in police custody after she was arrested on suspicion on fraud as she arrived back in the UK.

Anne Darwin was arrested at Manchester Airport after flying in overnight on Delta from Atlanta.

The 55-year-old was held at the airport for around three hours before she was driven back in a police convoy to the North East.

Cleveland Police said: "Anne Darwin is in police custody in Hartlepool Police Station where she is currently undergoing a medical examination before any formal interviews can take place."

Her 57-year-old husband is due to appear at Hartlepool Magistrates' Court.

The former prison officer has been charged with making an untrue statement to procure a passport and obtaining a money transfer by deception in relation to a life insurance policy.

He remains in police custody at Kirkleatham police station in Redcar, roughly 16 miles from where his wife was being held.

Police said an application would be made to remand him in custody, where he was said to be "fit and well" last night.

Mr Darwin was arrested at his son's house in Basingstoke, Hampshire, on suspicion of fraud and was then taken to the North East for questioning.

He disappeared from his home in Seaton Carew, near Hartlepool, in March 2002 and was presumed drowned after his wrecked canoe was found. But on December 1 he walked into a London police station, saying: "I think I am a missing person".
