U.S. Envoy Arrives in North Korea to Inspect Nuclear Plant

By Heejin Koo

Dec. 3 (Bloomberg) -- The top U.S. nuclear negotiator involved in talks to dismantle North Korea's atomic weapons program is in the communist nation for three days to assess progress made so far.

Assistant Secretary of State Christopher Hill told reporters last week he plans to visit North Korea's plutonium-producing plant in Yongbyon during the visit. He arrived in the capital, Pyongyang, today, China's official Xinhua News Agency reported.

North Korea faces a year-end deadline to disable the reactor and disclose its nuclear program under a six-nation accord. The U.S., China, South Korea, Japan and Russia pledged in turn to supply fuel oil or the equivalent in aid.

North Korea must include the number of nuclear warheads produced and the amount of processed plutonium in the declaration, Hill said last week. The declaration should take the form of a single statement, rather than come in several phases, Hill added.

Secretary of State Condoleezza Rice and Hill are battling skepticism from critics such as former U.S. Ambassador to the United Nations John Bolton that North Korea will fulfill its commitment to denuclearize.

Kim Jong Il's regime shut down the Yongbyon reactor in July and began disabling the plant earlier this month under the supervision of U.S. inspectors.

Hill, who last visited Pyongyang in June, didn't say whether he will return to Seoul or travel to Beijing following his visit to Yongbyon.

The chief negotiators involved in the six-nation talks will meet early or mid-December in Beijing and assess North Korea's progress in closing Yongbyon and its expected declaration of nuclear materials, South Korean Foreign Minister Song Min Soon told reporters on Nov. 27.
