Tennis-Fifth-seed Peer advances at Australian Hardcourt champs

Sun Dec 30, 2007 9:17am GMT
 
Email | Print |
Share
| Single Page |
[-] Text [+]

MELBOURNE, Dec 30 (Reuters) - Fifth-seeded Israeli Shahar Peer overcame a sluggish start before advancing to the second round of the Australian Hardcourt championships on the Gold Coast on Sunday.

The world number 17 dropped the first set before finally overcoming Julie Ditty of the United States 3-6 6-0 6-2 to set up a second-round meeting with the winner of the match between Lourdes Dominguez Lino and a qualifier.

"Julie was playing good and she's not even in the top 100," Peer told reporters. "I had a lot of trouble with her. Every match I have to play good."

There were only two other first round matches on Sunday with France's Nathalie Dechy beating China's Zi Yan 6-2 6-2, while Belarussia's Victoria Azarenka beat two-time Gold Coast champion Ai Sugiyama of Japan 6-2 6-3.

France's Amelie Mauresmo, the 2006 Australian Open and Wimbledon champion, will play her first round match on Monday against Olga Savchuk of Ukraine, while top seed Nicole Vaidisova of the Czech Republic will meet local wildcard Casey Dellacqua.

Vaidisova and Mauresmo are scheduled to meet in the semi-finals of the tournament that runs until Jan. 5.

The Australian Open, the year's first grand slam, runs from Jan. 14-27. (Writing by Greg Stutchbury; Editing by John O'Brien)
