Shaun Edwards on board with Wales

Jan 14 2008 by Andy Howell, Western Mail

SHAUN EDWARDS’ coaching role with Wales was rubber-stamped last night as Lawrence Dallaglio confirmed he could also be joining Warren Gatland’s revolution.

London Wasps director of rugby Ian McGeechan confirmed an agreement had been put in place to reunite Edwards with former club comrades Gatland and Rob Howley in international rugby.

The rugby league great will become Wales’ Six Nations defence guru with Blues assistant Howley becoming Wales’ backs coach. Both will be part-time roles.

The trio worked together as coaches and players at Wasps, delivering European and English League titles.

“It’s fine, it’s on-going, it’s moving on,” said McGeechan initially when questioned about the future of Edwards yesterday.

Warming to the theme, he expanded, “I think it will be sorted fairly quickly.

“Gats and I have spoken quite a lot over the last week. We have agreed between us how it will work.

“Yes, it is to our satisfaction. We will have to see what the final agreement is between the club and Wales, but the working agreement is sorted.

“The aim is to try and give Shaun an extra experience and bring knowledge back to the club. That’s how we will see it.

“But it won’t be doing anything detrimental to the club. That’s important.”

Former Lions and Scotland coach McGeechan declined to go into more detail about how the arrangement will work.

Gatland today reveals his first Wales squad since being appointed coach and the WRU is expected to officially announce it has recruited Edwards and Howley.

Former Wasps fitness chief Craig White is poised to join them in May, but Dallaglio could beat him to it – as reported in The Western Mail on Saturday.

The former England captain and Lions colossus confirmed last night, “I have had a discussion with Warren.

“The invite is there. It is a question of when to go down to Wales. I am certainly no threat to them any more now I have finished playing for England.

“I have always felt that to become a better coach you have to absorb as much knowledge as possible.

“That’s probably what Shaun is looking to do as well; he wants to become a better coach by coaching internationally.

“I have made no secret of the fact I want to get into coaching when I retire from playing at the end of the season.

“So I don’t see any harm in coming to Wales. It will be interesting to see how it all pans out on February 2.”

That is the day Wales open their bid for Six Nations glory, against England at Twickenham.

Dallaglio quipped, “I did say to Warren my retirement from international rugby means I had given up my chance to take him on as a player v coach!”

The no-nonsense back rower, who is likely to fill an informal mentoring role with Wales, added, “It is about sharing information and getting better.

“I am looking at coaching and Warren has never been too secretive about things he does.

“I have worked under Shaun and played alongside Rob, so it would be great to see what they do and what I could do.

“It would not be a case of me coming down to spy or spread the word, it would be about me just keeping abreast of what is going on at that level.”

Gatland, who was at the Wasps-Scarlets Heineken Cup tie yesterday, names his 28-man squad for the Six Nations with increasing worries over the fitness of scrum-half Dwayne Peel.

The Lions star has a shoulder injury and Gatland has warned he would not risk him against England if there was a danger of aggravating the problem.
