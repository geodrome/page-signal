Apple updates MacPros and Xserves, doubles performance.

  • 9th January 2008
  • Danny Gorog
  • Apple, PC Hardware

Apple today announced updates to its MacPro and Xserve line of computers, making sure the news has a week to itself before the major product announcements at Macworld.

While the new machines are monsters spec-wise (and, Apple claims, deliver up to twice the performance of their predecessors), they still don't include a Blu-ray option. Apple also failed to revamp its aging lineup of Cinema Displays, though fans are still hoping these will emerge at MacWorld expo in a week's time.

According to Apple, 'the new Mac Pro combines two of Intel’s new 45 nanometer Quad-Core Xeon processors running up to 3.2 GHz, powerful new graphics and up to 4TB of internal storage to offer the ideal system for creative professionals, 3D digital content creators and scientists. The standard 8-core configuration starts at just recommended retail price A$3,999 including GST' and is the fastest Mac Apple has ever shipped.

Whether there are really any applications out there that can take advantage of eight processor cores is another question altogether, of course.

The MacPro now ships standard with an ATI Radeon HD 2600 XT with 256MB of GDDR3 memory, PCI Express 2.0 and two dual-link DVI ports which gives dual 30" Apple Cinema Display support. An optional NVIDIA Quadro FX 5600 graphics card is available for $4050, with 1.5GB of GDDR3 memory and a 3D stereo port for stereo-in-a-window applications.

Apple also announced updates to its underloved Xserve range, which now ships as standard with a single 64-bit 2.8 GHz Quad-Core Xeon processor with 12MB of L2 cache and a 1600 Mhz front side bus, 2GB of 800 MHz DDR2 ECC FB-DIMM RAM, expandable up to 32GB, dual Gigabit Ethernet, internal graphics (to drive up to a 23-inch Apple Cinema Display), two Firewire 800 ports, three USB ports and an unlimited client license for Mac OS X Server version 10.5 Leopard for $4,499.

Apple offers numerous build-to-order options and accessories including dual 2.8 or 3.0 GHz Quad-Core Intel Xeon processors; 80GB and 1TB 7200 rpm SATA or 73GB or 300GB 15,000rpm SAS Apple Drive Modules; internal Xserve RAID card; Gigabit Ethernet, 4Gb Fibre Channel and U320 SCSI expansion card options, and a 750W redundant power supply.

Updated MacPros and Xserves are shipping immediately.
