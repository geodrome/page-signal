Police cruiser stolen at train station fire

Posted Jan. 5, 2008
3:47 PM

Fairfield (AP) _ Investigators looking into a fire that damaged a Metro-North commuter tail station in Fairfield last week also have a car-theft case on their hands.
  
Fairfield police say someone drove off in one of their cruisers while it was parked with its emergency lights flashing at the fire scene Thursday night.
  
The police car was recovered later in Westport, but without the keys.
  
Police say they are investigating whether there is any connection between the theft and the fire, which damaged the Metro-North westbound station in Fairfield's Southport section.
  
State officials are working to offer bus service and temporary shelter for commuters who use the terminal, which also housed an art gallery.
  
The art was not damaged in the fire.
