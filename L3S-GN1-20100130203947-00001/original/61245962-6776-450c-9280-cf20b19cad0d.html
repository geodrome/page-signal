First Test, day four, Port Elizabeth

Windies seal historic win



West Indies 408 & 175; South Africa 195 & 260
West Indies win by 128 runs

Staff and agencies
Saturday December 29, 2007
Guardian Unlimited


Fidel Edwards celebrates during the West Indies' historic victory over South Africa in the first Test of the series in Port Elizabeth. Photograph by Gianluigi Guercia / AFP.
 

West Indies beat South Africa by 128 runs in the first Test at Port Elizabeth to seal their first Test win in nearly three years.

Chasing a victory target of 389, the key moment came when Jacques Kallis was controversially given out caught behind off the bowling of Fidel Edwards for 85 when the ball appeared to clip his shoulder. The Proteas were eventually bowled out for 260, giving the Windies their first Test victory since they beat Pakistan in May 2005.

Resuming on 146 for eight on the morning of day four, the Windies were quickly cleaned up by left-armer Paul Harris as the tourists, who held a 213-run first innings lead, were dismissed for 175 in their second knock. Daren Powell did not last long, bowled by Harris for six, via an inside edge. Jerome Taylor contributed a feisty 22 from just 28 balls before he was caught at long-on by Andre Nel. Harris' double saw him finish the innings with figures of four for 35.

The South Africa chase got off to a terrible start as Powell and Fidel Edwards ripped through the top order to put the West Indies in control. At lunch, the home side had limped on to 45 for three. Powell set the ball rolling by trapping Herschelle Gibbs leg before wicket, with the batsman failing to offer a shot. It was Gibbs' second duck of the match. Edwards then got in on the act by claiming the wickets of Hashim Amla and South Africa captain Graeme Smith. A mla got an outside edge to wicketkeeper Denesh Ramdin having made eight, while Smith made just 11 before fending off a short delivery to Daren Ganga at short-leg. Kallis was the star in the afternoon session, stabilising the innings with a beautifully crafted knock after Ashwell Prince perished for 10.

Prince fell almost immediately after lunch, edging Taylor to Chris Gayle at first slip. But Kallis, aided by AB de Villiers, who was unbeaten on 31 at the interval, ensured they reached tea without further damage. The pair put on 112 for the fifth wicket, before Kallis departed after a questionable decision from umpire Russell Tiffin. The ball appeared to strike the all-rounder's shoulder before going through to Ramdin behind the stumps, but Tiffin raised his finger and Kallis departed for 85.

Taylor bowled Mark Boucher when the wicketkeeper played on for 13, before Dwayne Bravo removed Harris for a duck in almost identical fashion. De Villers (60) fell in tame fashion, chipping a Taylor delivery to Marlon Samuels at mid-on, before Nel (34) and Dale Steyn (33 not out) contributed some late-order runs with a stand of 67. But Darren Sammy accounted for Nel and Samuels claimed a wicket in the shape of Makhaya Ntini (one) to begin the tourists' celebrations.
