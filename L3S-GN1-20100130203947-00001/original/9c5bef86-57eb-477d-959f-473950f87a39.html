Rays Lock Up Pena With 3-year Deal

POSTED: 1:01 pm EST January 18, 2008

St. Petersburg, FL -- (Sports Network) - The Tampa Bay Rays agreed to terms with first baseman Carlos Pena on a one-year contract that can extend through 2010, pending the completion of a physical.

The contract would pay Pena $24.125 million over three years.

"It's awesome," Pena told The St. Petersburg Times from the Dominican Republic. "It's extremely exciting. I get to be in a place I absolutely love, around people that I love and people that I know care about me. It couldn't be any better. I'm pumped. We're celebrating."

The 29-year-old Pena was the consensus American League Comeback Player of the Year in 2007 after finishing with a team-record 46 home runs and 121 runs batted in. He was also awarded with an AL Silver Slugger Award, the first in Rays history.

Pena became just the 11th player in AL history to total 100 walks, 45 home runs and 120 RBI in a single season and his 46 homers eclipsed the Rays previous franchise record (34) by 12 and his previous career high (27) by 19.
