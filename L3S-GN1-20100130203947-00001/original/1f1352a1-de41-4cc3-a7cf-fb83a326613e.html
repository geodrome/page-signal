Dolphins players already feel a change in atmosphere with Parcells on the job

By TIM GRAHAM

Palm Beach Post Staff Writer

Thursday, December 27, 2007

DAVIE — Linebacker Joey Porter knew as soon as he pulled into the parking lot of the Dolphins' training facility Thursday that practice would be unusual.

He felt a little buzz the moment he saw all the television vans lined up. They were on the lookout for executive vice president of football operations Bill Parcells on his first day at work.

"Everybody's been waiting for Thursday," Porter told reporters. "We got the memo a long time ago that he was coming in on Thursday, so we were waiting just like you guys were waiting." Then Parcells emerged from the building and walked onto the practice fields.

There was no pageantry or pomp. Parcells, the only man who has coached four NFL franchises to the playoffs, observed quietly.

"I think the air in the practice field ... got a little thin," defensive end Vonnie Holliday said. "There was a little whisper here and there." Coach Cam Cameron exchanged pleasantries with Parcells, but they didn't speak for long. Parcells didn't address the team on the field.

Porter detected the team's intensity rise appreciably in Parcells' presence. Parcells will have authority over all football matters, including final say on who stays and who goes.

"Guys were nervous out there," Porter said. "They got to run around a little faster and everything. Coaches got to coach a little louder." Some players later would claim they didn't notice the future Hall of Famer; others said there was no way not to.

"Parcells, from the time he walked out, everybody knows he's there," Holliday said. "He has people's attention. He deserves it. That's what he's gained over the years after all those accomplishments.

"We all are excited about it. Guys hope to be a part of that. We know we have our work cut out for us and we're auditioning, and hopefully I'll be one of those guys in leading roles." Running back Samkon Gado felt Parcells' stare near the end of practice.

"I didn't know he was out there until it was almost over and I looked and he was standing right behind me," Gado said. "I was, like, ëOh, my goodness.' "People know he has a very imposing presence, and he's a very matter-of-fact man. He's going to say what he's going to say. He's going to do whatever he needs to do to get the job done. I just hope I'm on the good side of that."

The Dolphins are well aware that Parcells, who coached the New York Giants to a pair of Super Bowl victories, has a history of turning losers into winners.

He guided the New England Patriots (2-14 the season before he took over) to an AFC championship, led the New York Jets (1-15 the season before he took over) to the AFC title game and led the Dallas Cowboys (5-11 three straight seasons before he took over) to the playoffs.

"His track record speaks for itself," Gado said. "Wherever he's gone he's either set them up for great success or actually had great success." Said Porter: "Whatever it is he can do to come in to Miami to get this thing turned around, I'm all for it."
