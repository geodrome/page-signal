AT&T replaces batteries in U-verse cabinets after fires and explosions

Associated Press - January 16, 2008 12:55 PM ET

NEW YORK (AP) - AT&T says it's replacing backup batteries in 17,000 street-side cabinets after 4 of them exploded or burst in flames.

1 of them was in Cleveland.

The batteries provide backup power for the company's new "U-verse" TV service.

A telecom industry Web site says the first cabinet to explode was in Houston, in 2006. Another cabinet caught fire in Houston, while the other incidents were in Cleveland and Wauwatosa, Wisconsin.

An AT&T spokesman says no one was hurt by the malfunctioning lithium-metal-polymer batteries, but there was some property damage.
