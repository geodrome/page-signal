Mbeki puts top cop on leave
MPUMELELO MKHABELA Published:Jan 14, 2008

PRESIDENT Thabo Mbeki has granted National Police Commissioner Jackie Selebi “extended leave of absence”.

He did so after acting National Prosecuting Authority head Mokotedi Mpshe told Mbeki of the pending criminal prosecution of Selebi “a few days ago”.

Mbeki said yesterday that Selebi himself knew he could not stay in office once the NPA acted against him, as this would impact negatively on the police.

Mbeki met with the top leadership of the South African Police Service yesterday morning, and it was agreed that the country’s legal processes had to be respected.

“I f the NPA believes that it needs to proceed against the commissioner, we have to respect that to ensure that we do whatever is necessary to ensure that the law takes its course without hindrance from anybody,” he said.

“ The NPA will do their work as required by the law and the Constitution. So the law will take its course.”

Mbeki said since Mpshe told him a “few days ago” that the NPA would charge Selebi, the prosecutions chief had not confirmed whether he had yet done so .

“The NPA is sensitive to the fact that here you are dealing with the SAPS, which has got the responsibility to maintain law and order in the country and all sorts of matters that bear on national security. ”

Asked whether he had not seen any wrongdoing on the part of Selebi , Mbeki said: “I have said this before many times, that if anybody had information that the national commissioner had done wrong things, I would act on it. Nobody did. Nobody came with information.

“When the NPA came to me to say they were going to act, I said, Fine that’s your duty.”

Mbeki said it was “normal” for the NPA to submit its report on such matters to Justice Minister Brigitte Mabandla.

Mabandla had informed him that the NPA’s review report would recommend proceeding with charges against Selebi .

Mbeki said he did not see why the Selebi matter should impact on the Ginwala Commission set up last year following the suspension of National Director of Public Prosecutions, Vusi Pikoli.

In what was seen as an attempt to block Selebi’s prosecution, Pikoli was suspended due to “an irretrievable breakdown in the relationship” between him and his boss, Mabandla.
