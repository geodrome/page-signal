Djokovic strong and healthy for Australian tilt

Thu Jan 17, 2008 5:51pm IST
 
Email | Print |
Share
| Single Page
[-] Text [+]

By Ossian Shine

MELBOURNE (Reuters) - Exhaustion ruined his season-ending Masters Cup but Novak Djokovic is making sure energy levels will not be an issue at the Australian Open.

The third seed sailed past Italy's Simone Bolelli 6-1 6-2 6-2 to reach the third round on Thursday, having dispatched first-round victim Benjamin Becker for the loss of just eight games.

"I feel good," the Serb said. "I had a long season last year, so I was trying to rest and prepare well.

"I didn't play any tournaments before the Australian Open, and that's why now, physically, I feel pretty good."

Next up for Djokovic is American Sam Querrey, conqueror of 32nd seed Dmitry Tursunov.

"Everybody (who) plays a grand slam, there's 128 players from a lot of countries, a lot of different countries coming here with a special motivation to play and to win.

"It's always dangerous these first two, three rounds at the start of the season."
