Bay Area DUI Arrests Down

POSTED: 2:35 pm PST December 30, 2007

Bay Area California Highway Patrol Officers arrested 103 drunken drivers between 6 p.m. Friday and 6 a.m. today during the agency's New Year's Maximum Enforcement Period, officials announced today.

The number of Bay Area drunken driving arrests is down this year compared to the same period in 2006, when 120 drivers were arrested on suspicion of driving under the influence of drugs or alcohol, according to the CHP.

No fatal collisions occurred in the Bay Area during the enforcement period this year or during the same reporting period in 2006, the CHP reported.

Statewide, CHP drunken driving arrests are up this year compared to the same reporting period in 2006.

CHP officers have arrested 806 drivers on suspicion of drunken driving during the enforcement period so far, up from the 796 arrests for drunken driving made during the same period in 2006, according to officials.

Statewide traffic deaths, reported from CHP and local police agencies, are down this year compared to 2006, with 13 people killed in the state so far during the maximum enforcement period compared to 17 people killed in traffic accidents during the same period in 2006, according to the CHP.

Numbers reported during the New Year's Maximum Enforcement Period are provisional, as not all agencies have reported. Final results will be compiled at the program's end, according to the CHP.

The CHP's New Year's Maximum Enforcement Period will continue through midnight Tuesday, the CHP reported.
