Bad news comes in threes

  • Font Size: Decrease Increase
  • Print Page: Print

Malcolm Conn | January 10, 2008

REPORTED spinner Brad Hogg faces a triple dose of bad news as Australia's selectors prepare to name the team for the third Test against an agitated India, beginning in Perth on Wednesday.

And Matthew Hayden remains in doubt with a hamstring tendon strain, but will travel to Perth on Saturday as part of the team schedule for a medical examination.

Australia's team physiotherapist Alex Kountouris rated Hayden only a 50-50 chance of playing, possibly opening the way for prolific West Australian opener Chris Rogers to make his Test debut.

Not only has Hogg been reported under the same International Cricket Council code of conduct charge as suspended Indian spinner Harbhajan Singh, the West Australian is suffering a finger injury and is in danger of losing his place to tearaway fast bowler Shaun Tait.

Hogg is expected to be named in an unchanged XII today but his selection may be academic, with a code of conduct hearing tentatively set for Monday.

He has been reported by team manager Chetan Chauhan for directing the word "bastard" at India captain Anil Kumble and vice-captain MS Dhoni on the final day of the second Test in Sydney last Sunday.

The alleged offence has been reported under paragraph 3.3 of the ICC code of conduct which refers to players or team officials using language or gestures that offends, insults, humiliates, intimidates, threatens, disparages or vilifies another person on the basis of that person's race, religion, gender, colour, descent, or national or ethic origin.

The penalty for a level three offence is a ban of between two and four Test matches or between four and eight one-day games.

Even if Hogg is dropped or is in line to be 12th man, which appears the case in Perth, any penalty will be served during the next match or matches.

The decision by the Indians to report Hogg for a word which has been used numerous times on an international cricket field raises more onerous standards.

Hogg is battling to keep his place in the Test side despite scoring a vital 79 during the first innings in Sydney.

Spinners took six of the 10 Indian wickets to fall on the final day, but Hogg did not take one.

Andrew Symonds claimed 3-51 with 19 overs of off-spin and earnt special praise from captain Ricky Ponting, while Michael Clarke's left-arm finger spin turned the match on its head with three wickets in five balls to claim a most improbable victory.

Hogg also has an injured little finger on his right hand, his non-bowling hand, but Kountouris said scans had cleared Hogg of any break.

However, his failure to make an impact in Sydney gives the selectors the chance to squeeze Tait into the side.

The raw-boned South Australian was frighteningly quick in last month's Twenty20 match against New Zealand in Perth, where he claimed two wickets with his first four balls.

He also had a productive Chappell-Hadlee Trophy series against the Kiwis but was left out of the Test side for Mitchell Johnson, who made a successful debut against Sri Lanka in November.

Likely team: Matthew Hayden or Chris Rogers, Phil Jaques, Ricky Ponting (c), Mike Hussey, Michael Clarke, Andrew Symonds, Adam Gilchrist, Brett Lee, Mitchell Johnson, Stuart Clark, Shaun Tait, Brad Hogg likely 12th man if not suspended
