Research confirms prairie grass could be good source of ethanol

Associated Press - January 8, 2008 7:04 PM ET

OMAHA, Neb. (AP) - The new switch grass study adds to the evidence supporting the development of cellulosic ethanol.

That's according to Renewable Fuels Association Matt Hartwig. He says ethanol created from cellolose-based fuels will be important to satisfying the nation's energy needs in the future.

The energy bill will emphasize cellulosic ethanol, made from such feedstock as switch grass and wood chips, after 2015 when about two-thirds of the nation's ethanol is supposed to come from such non-corn sources.

By 2022, the law calls for 36 billion gallons of ethanol a year.

Hartwig says there is general agreement that 15 billion gallons a year is about the most ethanol that can be produced from grain with current technology without hurting grain markets. So he said it's important to develop other sources for the renewable fuel.

The study was conducted by the University of Nebraska and USDA.
