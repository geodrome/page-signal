'Alien vs. Predator: Requiem' is a true horror
 

What says "the holiday season" better than "Aliens vs. Predator"?

Well ... um ... ANYTHING.

It's not that such a movie doesn't have its place during the Christmas-New Year's period. You'd just like it to be good.

Like an earlier film that merged the two successful horror-adventure franchises, "Aliens vs. Predator: Requiem" takes the monsters involved and pits them against each other. A no-brainer, right?

Actually, that's true in more than one sense. What made the original, separate "Alien" and "Predator" movies work was the emphasis on human characters as well as the creatures. Now, it's the same as any other watered-down idea: Take the bare basics, pour them on heavier, and hope they still work.

This time, aliens crash-land in Colorado and a predator follows them there. The easy solution for anyone in the line of fire? Relocate to another state -- fast! -- but then you'd have a two-minute movie. Which, under the circumstances, wouldn't be a bad option.

"Aliens vs. Predator: Requiem" delivers precisely what you would expect: gore, gore and more gore. And we're not talking Al and Tipper. Worse still, some good actors get mired in the proceedings, such as familiar television faces Reiko Aylesworth ("24") and Robert Joy ("CSI: NY").

Just the title guarantees a ready-made audience, so the makers could afford to take some creative risks and still make their profit. But there's not a chance of that in evidence here. You know virtually everything about the movie before you walk in the theater.

That is the picture's true terror.

(Rated R)
