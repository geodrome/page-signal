Shuttle Launch Scrubbed
 
Thursday, Dec 27, 2007 - 09:43 PM

NASA has scrubbed the launch of the shuttle Atlantis indefinitely until they can fix the problem with fuel sensors.

George Diller - NASA "We've been plagued with this now for more than two years. And, when this happened this time around after we thought we'd fixed it before, we decided we were gonna get to the root cause of this problem and fix it once and for all."

Tests using super cold rocket fuel have given engineers a good idea of what's going wrong on. Atlantis and other shuttles that've had the same problem.

It looks like a bad connection between sensors inside the shuttle's fuel tank and computers in the shuttle. So, at the launch pad, technichicans are sawing through foam insulation to get at the bad connectors. They hope to have them out by Saturday.

More tests at NASA's Marshall Space Flight center in Alabama could confirm the suspicion that the connection can be fixed by soldering the part instead of just plugging them in like a lamp in a wall socket.

NASA had hoped to launch Atlantis by January 10th. Many new pieces of the International Space Station are waiting on the ground for the shuttle's to fly them into place before the end of 2010. And, Space Station Astronaut Dan Tani, who's mother died in an accident, is waiting to get home. He would have been already, if Atlantis had launched on time.

George Diller - NASA "If we're going do 13 more launches of the space shuttle, we don't want to keep fooling with this. We want to fix it. so, that's why we're taking extra time and doing it the way we think is going to fix it permanently."
