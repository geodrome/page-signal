Thai market bombing injures 39

Staff and agencies

15 January, 2008







By SUMETH PANPETCH, Associated Press Writer 42 minutes ago

YALA, Thailand - Suspected Muslim insurgents exploded a bomb that left at least 39 people injured in a southern Thai market Tuesday, officials said, a day after rebels ambushed a military patrol and killed eight soldiers.

The explosion was the third blow for the government in as many days, coming after Monday?s ambush ? during which one soldier was beheaded ? and a jailbreak Sunday that saw six suspected militants escape.

An army spokesman said the Monday ambush was a reaction to what he claimed was the military?s progress in tracking down rebel leaders.

Attackers who had been hiding in brush by the roadside emerged and fired a barrage of bullets, leaving no survivors, said police Lt. Col. Chakkrote Nongmanee.

More than 30 people, many of them civilians, have been decapitated during the insurgency. The object appears to be to terrorize Buddhists into leaving the region.

The region was a sultanate until it was annexed by Thailand in the early 1900s, and most of its Muslim residents are ethnic Malays who have more in common with the people of neighboring Malaysia.
