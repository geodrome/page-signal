NHL tweaks rules for first US outdoor game

12-30-2007, 02h53
BUFFALO, New York (AFP)




Making allowances for possible rink advantages in the first outdoor game played on US ice, the National Hockey League announced rule modifications Saturday when Pittsburgh and Buffalo meet.

NHL star Sidney Crosby will lead the Penguins against Buffalo, whose loyal fans will brave sub-freezing cold and possible wind and snow to watch their team face an Eastern Conference rival on New Year's Day.

The league announced the Sabres and Penguins will switch sides midway through the third period, something common in global play but not done in NHL games.

Teams would also switch rink ends after 2:30 of sudden-death overtime, then play another 2:30 to complete the usual NHL overtime period as needed.

Should a shootout be necessary to decide a winner, each goaltender will be able to choose which goal they defend in the one-on-one session.

Weather conditions might also force the league to modify the length of intermissions and timeouts.

The changes were made to nullify any possible competitive edge that weather conditions might produce, an issue not found in the league's usual arenas in US and Canadian cities.

Two college games have been played outdoors in US stadiums but the only prior NHL game staged outdoors was between the Montreal Canadiens and Edmonton Oilers on November 22, 2003 at Edmonton's Commonwealth Stadium.

The Heritage Classic drew 57,167, the largest live crowd to watch an NHL game, despite severe cold and winds.

Weather conditions could force the game to be postponed to Wednesday, a contingency for which the league has prepared even though it would diminish the relatively huge holiday US television exposure the NHL expects.
