Premier League Friday, Jan 11, 2008 
Portsmouth's Redknapp favorite for Toon job  


By Associated Press



NEWCASTLE, England -- Portsmouth manager Harry Redknapp emerged as the favorite to take over from Sam Allardyce at Newcastle, while Blackburn's Mark Hughes said he doesn't expect the Magpies to move for him.

Allardyce left the club after only eight months in charge on Wednesday, and there was speculation that Newcastle would make an official request Friday to Portsmouth to sign the 60-year-old Redknapp.

Portsmouth will only be a few miles (kilometers) from Newcastle on Sunday when it visits Sunderland in the Premier League. Redknapp did not appear at Portsmouth's scheduled news conference, which was instead taken by his assistant Joe Jordan.

"We had a little bit of a laugh and a joke about all the speculation this morning as is his way," the former Manchester United and Leeds striker said. "It's no surprise that the manager of Portsmouth is considered on that short list. Portsmouth are doing very well and that that's down to the man who picks the team and brings the players in."

Jordan said he knew of no plans for Redknapp to talk to Newcastle.

"Things will go on as planned in preparation for the (Sunderland) game," he said. "The chief executive, Peter Storrie, and Harry have gone away and I think their duties today are to talk to a player who hopefully they can persuade to come to Fratton Park."

Although Redknapp has not won any major titles, he had a moderately successful spell in charge of West Ham and helped Portsmouth gain promotion to the Premier League in 2003. Moving to Southampton, he failed to stop the Saints being relegated for the first time in 27 years before returning to Portsmouth in December 2005.

This season, Pompey has consistently been in the top half and won 4-1 at Newcastle in November.

Newcastle is preparing for a Premier League game against defending champion Manchester United, whose manager, Alex Ferguson, says he is amazed at the lack of titles for the Magpies considering the number of big-name coaches that have been at St. James' Park.

"The expectation levels at Newcastle are too great, a lot of people are saying that now. When you analyze the situation up there, it is beyond belief," Ferguson said.

"They have had two managers who have won World Cups as players - Jack Charlton and Ossie Ardiles - four who have won the European Cups as players - Kevin Keegan, Kenny Dalglish, Ruud Gullit and Graeme Souness - and Sir Bobby Robson, who took England to the World Cup semifinals.

"You would think one of them would have won something given the right time. I don't know what you can say about the situation up there, but it is a strange club."

Hughes is another leading candidate being touted in the media, but the Blackburn manager said he had received no contact from Newcastle.

"There's always speculation when any big job comes up," the former Manchester United and FC Barcelona striker said. "I seem to be talked about in good terms and I'm appreciative of that. But it's just recognition of the work myself and my staff do here.

"I have not spoken to anyone from Newcastle. I spoke to my chairman last night and he has had no contact, and I don't think there will be any contact."

Hughes, who also had a spell in charge of Wales, has been with Rovers since September 2004 and led the team to a sixth-place finish in the Premier League two years later. Blackburn is ninth in the standings this season, two places higher than Newcastle and one behind Portsmouth.

"I am in a very strong position here. I have a great relationship with the chairman and the board and that is really important," Hughes said. "When managers have that relationship it's a big decision to forgo that, as the relationship is the key to whether we can be successful."
