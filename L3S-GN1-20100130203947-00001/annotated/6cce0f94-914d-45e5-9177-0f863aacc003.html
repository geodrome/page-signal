Ticket registration for Glastonbury







« Previous
« Previous
Next »
Next »


View Gallery
Revellers hoping for a ticket to this summer's Glastonbury Festival will have to register their interest next month, organiser Michael Eavis said.
As part of the battle against touts, anyone wanting a ticket to the world's biggest music and performing arts festival must register by the end of February.



Mr Eavis, the boss of the Somerset extravaganza, said: "I want to keep up the momentum we gathered last year by stopping ticket touting altogether. Thank you for being so supportive of our registration process, which is, after all, quite tedious. I appreciate it so much."



The first Glastonbury Festival was held on September 19 1970, the day after Jimi Hendrix died. Around 1,500 people came and the ticket price was Â£1 (including free milk from the farm).



The attendance in 2005 was 153,000. Tickets were Â£125 including a programme.



The 2008 registration process is similar to last year's system, which involved supplying contact details and a standard passport photo.



Everyone, including children aged 13 and over, who wants to buy tickets will need to register first to be eligibl
e to go through the sales procedure on April 6. Each ticket sold will feature a photograph of the person in whose name it is registered and will be non-transferable.



The registration window is open between 9am on Friday February 1 and midnight on Friday February 29.



Music lovers can either sign up online - via www.glastonburyfestivals.co.uk - or by completing a registration form, which will be available from any branch of Millets, the high street camping store, or by sending a stamped addressed envelope to Glastonbury Registration, Millets, Mansard Close, Westgate, Northampton NN5 5DL.



Registration does not reserve or guarantee a ticket but failure to register will make it impossible to get a weekend ticket for Glastonbury Festival 2008 which takes place at Worthy Farm, Pilton over the weekend of June 27-29.
