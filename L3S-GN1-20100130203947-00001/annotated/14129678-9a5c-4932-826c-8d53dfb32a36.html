Alouettes add two to coaching staff

Montreal, QC (Sports Network) - The Montreal Alouettes named Scott Milanovich offensive coordinator and Vince Martino offensive line coach on Wednesday.

Milanovich joined the Als last season as quarterbacks coach after four seasons in NFL Europe with Cologne and the Rhein Fire. The 34-year-old previously saw time in the NFL, CFL, AFL, NFL Europe and the XFL as a quarterback.

Martino comes to Montreal after guiding the Hamburg Sea Devils to a World Bowl championship in 2007. The 60-year-old native of upstate New York began his coaching career with Boston College, and also coached with North Central College and the University of Maine.

The Alouettes finished with an 8-10 record in 2007, good enough for third place in the East Division and a postseason defeat to the Winnipeg Blue Bombers. The franchise heads also into 2008 with a new head coach, Marc Trestman.
