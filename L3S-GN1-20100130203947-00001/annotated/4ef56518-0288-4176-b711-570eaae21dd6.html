Fresh warning from bin Laden

  • Email
  • Printer friendly version
  • Normal font
  • Large font
Roland Parry, Washington

December 31, 2007

AL-QAEDA chief Osama bin Laden has warned Muslims against supporting Iraq's US-backed Government and promised the "liberation of Palestine", in an online message.

In the 56-minute tape, the hunted militant accused the US of seeking to control the region through the Iraqi Government, according to SITE, a US institute that monitors extremist web forums.

He singled out Iraqis fighting al-Qaeda in Anbar province, and the Islamic Party of Iraq, a political group allied with the Government, saying Muslims who support it are traitors to Islam.

"The group is assisting the Americans in Iraq" and "fighting against the mujahideen" Islamist warriors, bin Laden said, according to an excerpt translated from the Arabic by SITE.

The message was released on Saturday in the form of a video displaying a still image of bin Laden along with an audio commentary.

Bin Laden labelled any Muslims co-operating with the US-led Iraqi Government of Prime Minister Nuri al-Maliki as apostates, and accused the US of seeking to set up a separate Iraqi government through which to access oil.

He also said that with Islamist fighters currently engaged in Iraq, the "liberation of Palestine" will follow, adding that the mujahideen would never recognise Israel nor any Palestinian government that accepted a Jewish government, including one led by the Hamas Islamist movement.

The terror leader's message coincided with a statement by the top US commander in Iraq saying al-Qaeda is becoming increasingly fearful over losing the support of Sunni Arabs and has begun targeting the leaders of tribal councils who have switched allegiances in favour of America.

General David Petraeus made the comments in Baghdad a few hours before the audio tape emerged. The tape included a warning from bin Laden to Iraq's Sunni Arabs against joining the councils fighting al-Qaeda or participating in any form of unity government.

The White House said the al-Qaeda message showed that the situation in Iraq was improving, with Iraqis turning against al-Qaeda.

AFP, AP
