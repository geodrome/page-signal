Roddick swings into the Fourth Round

  • Font Size: Decrease Increase
  • Print Page: Print

January 17, 2008

DESPITE a performance which featured 75 per cent of his first serves in and 34 clear winners, Andy Roddick found himself sheepishly talking about two fresh-air swings after cruising into the third round of the Australian Open tonight.

Sixth seed Roddick brushed aside unseeded German Michael Berrer 6-2 6-2 6-4 in just under two hours with little fuss on Rod Laver Arena.

So little fuss in fact, that all the post-match talk was about two "whiffs" Roddick made during an otherwise tradesman-like second round performance - one on a rare break point for his outclassed opponent.

After Roddick spent two sets cooking Berrer in a broth of his own unforced errors, the German was thrown a morsel - a service break in the third set on a mishit by the American.

Roddick was so disgusted he bounced his racquet with feeling after conceding the game - one of few moments he actually had to expend excess energy, so in control was he of the match.

Normal service was soon resumed when Roddick broke back soon after, though he did take another air swing in the set as the balls slowed on the new Plexicushion surface.

"You know what sucks is when you have to talk about two whiffs as opposed to one - I just didn't want the first one to be lonely," Roddick said.

"The second one I just sucked, and went swing and a miss."

Roddick admitted the new surface at Melbourne Park had a tendency to be slower than the old Rebound Ace, though he felt he coped well with the court and balls which moved slower as a result of becoming more worn.

"It's (Plexicushion's) a good bit slower. The simplest analogy is if you have a piece of sandpaper and rub a ball on it, it's going to get this way a lot faster than if I rub it on this table and it's smooth.

"That's the effect the surface is having on the ball. It's normally a welcome change when they say 'new balls'."

Roddick, who has never made it past the semi-finals at Melbourne Park, now plays experienced German Philipp Kohlschreiber in the third round.

 

- AAP
