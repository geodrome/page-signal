White Scores 22 Points In Women's Basketball Loss
Courtesy: San Jose State Athletics

          Release: 01/10/2008

San Jose State-Nevada statistics
 
Courtesy: San Jose State Athletics



Tamiko Davis scored a career high eight points for the Spartans Thursday night. (Photo by Damon Tarver)

Reno, Nev.? Natalie White recorded her seventh career 20-plus point game for San Jose State, but Nevada used a 12-0 run in the first half to take control of the game to win 72-51, Thursday night in a Western Athletic Conference game at the Lawlor Events Center.

White scored a team-best 22 points and was 11-for-17 from the field.  Ironically, the Spartans? top three-point shooter on the season was 0-for-2 from three-point range.  White added five rebounds and an assist.

Spartan Tamiko Davis scored a career best eight points and grabbed seven rebounds.  Myosha Barnes was the other Spartan in double figures with 10 points including 4-of-6 from the field and 2-for-2 from the free throw line.  Brittany Powell led San Jose State in rebounding with eight and added five points.

The Spartans again started the first half strong for the second consecutive game.  After Nevada took an early 6-2 lead, San Jose State got three consecutive made baskets by White to take an 8-6 lead at 17:07, and held the Wolf Pack scoreless for almost five minutes.

?We have to learn that our defensive play dictates our offense? said San Jose State head coach Pam DeCosta .  ?We can get a lead in games, but one or two missed shots offensively, and we start to hang our heads and not play good defense.

?We also took some quick shots in the first half which allowed Nevada to run down the court and get easy baskets on their end.  We are still learning that even if we miss a shot, we get play good defense and get a stop.  Then we can score just as easily.?

However, a made three-pointer by Johnna Ward at 13:45 gave Nevada the lead for good at 10-8.  The Wolf Pack then held San Jose State scoreless for the next eight minutes and jumped out to a 22-8 lead.  The Spartans cut into the lead and got as close as 11 at 27-16, but Nevada used a 14-3 run the final six minutes to lead 41-19 at halftime.

San Jose State is now 1-14 overall and 0-2 in the WAC, while Nevada won its conference opener and improves to 10-4 overall and 1-0 in the WAC.  The Spartans return to action on Saturday, January 19, with the WAC home opener against Hawaii.  Game time is set for 7 p.m., in The Event Center.
